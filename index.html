About Opossums

Opossums are marsupials that are native to the Americas. They are the only marsupials that are found in the United States and Canada. Opossums are known for their unique behaviors and physical characteristics. They are often misunderstood and underappreciated animals. Opossums are beneficial to the environment because they eat insects, rodents, and other pests. They are also immune to rabies and are resistant to snake venom. Opossums are solitary animals that are nocturnal and are most active at night. They are excellent climbers and are known for their ability to escape from predators by climbing trees. Opossums are also known for their ability to play dead when they are threatened. They are gentle animals that are not aggressive and are not dangerous to humans. Opossums are intelligent animals that are capable of learning and problem-solving. They are social animals that are capable of forming bonds with other opossums. Opossums are fascinating animals that are worthy of our respect and admiration.
